## Forget the Magic Tricks

Founders often ask me how to trick artificial intelligence into recommending their software. They think I have a secret backdoor to the Gemini algorithm. I do not. No one does. 

Getting an AI to recommend your business is not a magic trick. It is just digital plumbing. You have to clean out the pipes before the water can flow clearly. 

If you want a primer on the theory you can read the extensive Generative Engine Optimization coverage over at [Search Engine Land](https://searchengineland.com). They are a massive digital marketing publication and their core advice aligns perfectly with reality. AI models need clean facts to operate securely. 

You do not need an advanced computer science degree to start fixing your footprint. Here is exactly where you begin.

### Step One: Audit Your Existing Facts

Before you change anything on your website you must understand your baseline. Open ChatGPT and Perplexity. Ask them ten direct questions about your brand. 

* Who are your main competitors?
* What is your base pricing tier?
* Where is your headquarters located?

If the models give you the wrong answers you have a data leak. You cannot build a strategy until you map every single hallucination. Find out exactly what the machine gets wrong.

### Step Two: Deploy Clean Schema

Generative engines do not want to read your marketing copy. They want to read your backend code. They look for JSON LD schema. This is a specific coding language that labels your business data perfectly. 

You need to wrap your business name, your address, your pricing, and your executive team in this exact code. When the AI crawler hits your site it immediately ingests those verified facts. It eliminates the guesswork.

### Step Three: Simplify Your Pages

Stop writing giant blocks of text. AI models extract information by reading headings and bullet points. 

If your service page has four paragraphs of fluffy text before it explains what you actually do the AI will abandon the page. You must place your core value proposition in the first sentence. 

Use clear bold headings. Use numbered lists. Make it incredibly easy for a machine to parse your page and steal your answer. That is the entire goal. 

You do not need to hire a prompt engineer. You just need to organize your data.
